Lal Bahadur Shastri


Lal Bahadur Shastri (1904-1966) was an influential Indian statesman and the second Prime Minister of India. Here's some key information about him:

Early Life and Education: Lal Bahadur Shastri was born on October 2, 1904, in Mughalsarai, a small town in present-day Uttar Pradesh, India. He came from a humble background and faced financial constraints during his early education.

Involvement in the Independence Movement: Shastri was actively involved in India's struggle for independence from British colonial rule. He participated in various movements and protests, including Mahatma Gandhi's Non-Cooperation Movement and the Civil Disobedience Movement. He was jailed several times for his participation in these movements

Political Career: After India gained independence in 1947, Shastri entered politics. He held various ministerial positions in independent India, including the role of Minister of Railways. His dedication and effective administration in this role earned him respect and recognition.
