Bhagat Singh


Bhagat Singh (1907-1931) was a prominent Indian freedom fighter and revolutionary who played a significant role in the struggle for India's independence from British colonial rule.

Bhagat Singh is best known for his fearless and principled acts of protest against British oppression. He became a symbol of resistance and defiance, particularly after he and his associates threw non-lethal bombs in the Central Legislative Assembly in Delhi in 1929, seeking to draw attention to the repressive laws imposed by the British.

Tragically, Bhagat Singh, along with Rajguru and Sukhdev, was sentenced to death for his involvement in the killing of a British police officer. On March 23, 1931, at the age of 23, he was hanged in Lahore Central Jail, but his legacy lived on as a source of inspiration for countless generations of Indians in their quest for freedom.
