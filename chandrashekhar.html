Chandrashekhar Azad


Chandrashekhar Azad, born on July 23, 1906, in Bhavra, India, was a prominent Indian revolutionary and freedom fighter who played a vital role in the fight against British colonial rule. He was a member of the Hindustan Socialist Republican Association (HSRA) and adopted the pseudonym "Azad," meaning "free" in Hindi, to signify his commitment to India's independence.

Azad was involved in the Kakori Conspiracy of 1925, where he and his associates attempted to loot a train carrying government funds to finance revolutionary activities. He evaded capture by British authorities for several years and was known for his daring acts against the colonial regime.

Tragically, he chose to die by his own hand during a shootout with the police on February 27, 1931, in Allahabad, rather than be captured alive.
