Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel


Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel (1875-1950), commonly known as Sardar Patel, was a prominent leader in India's struggle for independence from British colonial rule and a key figure in the country's post-independence period. Here's a brief overview of his life and contributions:

Patel was born on October 31, 1875, in Nadiad, Gujarat, India. He came from a humble background and pursued a career in law, becoming a successful barrister in England.

Patel was a staunch follower of Mahatma Gandhi and actively participated in various aspects of the Indian independence movement. He played a crucial role in organizing peasants and farmers during movements such as the Kheda and Bardoli Satyagrahas, where he successfully negotiated with the British authorities to secure concessions for the Indian farmers.
